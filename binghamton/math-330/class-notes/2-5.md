> **Prop. 2.2.**  
> For $m\in\mathbb{Z}$, one and only one of the following is true:
> - $m\in\mathbb{N}$
> - $-m\in\mathbb{N}$

Proof.  
Let $m\in\mathbb{Z}$.
Part 1: Prove $m\in\mathbb{N} \land -m\in\mathbb{N}$ cannot be true.
